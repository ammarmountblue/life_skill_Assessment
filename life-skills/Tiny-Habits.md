# Tiny Habits - Assignment Answers



## **1. Most Interesting Idea from Tiny Habits Video (BJ Fogg)**

The most interesting idea was how tiny actions, like just doing two push-ups a day, can slowly grow into big habits. It showed that small changes are easier to start and can lead to big results.



## **2. How B = MAP Helps Build Habits**

B = MAP means **Behavior = Motivation + Ability + Prompt**

- **M = Motivation** (your desire to do it)  
- **A = Ability** (how easy or hard it is)  
- **P = Prompt** (a trigger or reminder)

To make habits easier, keep motivation small but make the habit **easy to do** and **create a clear reminder** so you don’t forget.



## **3. Why “Shine” or Celebrate?**

Celebrating after doing a habit makes your brain feel happy.  
This happiness makes your brain want to do it again.  
Whatever you celebrate becomes stronger, so celebrating helps habits stick.



## **4. Most Interesting Idea from 1% Better Everyday Video**

The idea that improving by just **1% every day** leads to huge results over time was interesting. It teaches that small progress daily is more powerful than big changes once in a while.



## **5. Book’s Perspective About Identity (Atomic Habits)**

The book says habits start with **identity first**.  
Instead of saying “I want to run,” think “I am a runner.”  
When your identity changes, your actions naturally follow.



## **6. How to Make a Habit Easier (Book Perspective)**

- Make it simple  
- Reduce the steps  
- Prepare in advance  
- Start very small  

When a habit is easy, you don’t resist doing it.



## **7. How to Make a Habit Harder (Book Perspective)**

- Increase the effort needed  
- Create friction  
- Remove tools that make it easy  
- Add steps so you avoid doing it

Making something hard reduces the chances of doing it.


## **8. Habit I Want to Do More**

**Habit:** Reading 10 minutes daily  

**Steps:**

- Keep my book on my desk so it’s visible  
- Read after dinner (fixed cue)  
- Use a small goal (just 2 pages)  
- Celebrate by smiling or ticking it off in a tracker  



## **9. Habit I Want to Reduce**

**Habit:** Spending too much time on phone/social media  

**Steps:**

- Keep phone in another room while studying (cue invisible)  
- Turn off notifications (less attractive)  
- Use app limits (harder to access)  
- Track usage and feel accountable (less satisfying)
